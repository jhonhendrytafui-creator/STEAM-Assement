## C1: STEAM Project Screening Rubric

### Decision Logic (Total Score: 40 Points)
* **32 – 40 Points:** **Accepted.** The project is well-defined and shows strong STEAM potential.
* **22 – 31 Points:** **Accepted with Revision.** The idea is good, but the student needs to clarify the problem, solution, or specific STEAM concepts before starting.
* **Below 22 Points:** **Disapproved.** The project lacks depth, is not a "problem-solving" project, or does not meet the STEAM integration requirements.

| | **Depth & Logic** | Explanation is too brief or lacks any logical reasoning. | Explanation is clear but lacks evidence or a "cause-and-effect" structure. | The problem is well-explained with a logical flow from cause to impact. | The problem is analyzed deeply with clear evidence and a highly structured argument. |

Proposed Solution** | **Rationale & Choice** | No reason is given for choosing the solution. | The reason for the choice is weak or based only on "ease of making." | Explains why the solution was chosen based on its ability to solve the problem. | Provides a compelling, evidence-based argument for why this is the best solution. |

### Additions for your "STEAM Expert" Perspective:

1.  **Significance (Dimension 2):** In STEAM, we don't just build for the sake of building. I added this to ensure students aren't just "making a volcano" but are actually solving a "pain point" in their lives.
2.  **Feasibility (Dimension 3):** Many students propose "flying cars" or "cancer-curing apps." As an expert, I added this to ensure they can actually finish the project with the tools they have.
3.  **Interdisciplinary Link (Dimension 4):** This is the "secret sauce" of STEAM. It's not enough to list "Math" and "Science." This indicator checks if the student understands how Math *helps* the Science, or how Art *improves* the Engineering.
